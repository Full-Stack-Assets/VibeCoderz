## The problem (section)

You're either overpaying — defaulting every question to the most expensive
model — or babysitting a model picker and guessing. Both waste money. A
two‑line summary shouldn't cost the same as architecting a distributed system.

## How it works (3 steps)

1. **You ask.** Anything — debug code, research a topic, analyze a CSV, read a screenshot.
2. **The COO engine routes.** It scores the task on type, required quality, latency, and your remaining budget, then picks the cost‑optimal model that clears the bar.
3. **You see the receipt.** Which model ran, the routing reason, a fit score, and the exact cost — every turn.

## FAQ

- **Does cheaper mean worse?** No — the router only downshifts when the cheaper model clears the quality bar for that task. Hard tasks still get premium models.
- **Can I force a specific model?** Yes, on Pro/Max you can pin any model or raise the quality floor.
- **Will I get a surprise bill?** No. You set a spend cap; it's enforced server‑side. Hit it and you can top up — never auto‑charged past your cap.
- **Which models?** A current frontier catalog across Anthropic, OpenAI, Google, and xAI — kept up to date.
- **Is the savings claim real?** It's measured. The benchmark harness is open; run it yourself.
